QFocus: Controllable Synthesis for Automated Speech Stress Editing to Deliver Human-Like Emphatic Intent

Jingyi Fang, Yufei Tang, Zhiyu Wu, Yuanzhong Zheng, Yaoxuan Wang, Haojun Fei

Qifu Technology, Inc.

Abstract

Recent advances in Text-to-Speech (TTS) have enabled highly natural speech synthesis, yet fine-grained para-language control, particularly prosodic emphasis editing remains a significant challenge. Existing approaches still struggle to achieve natural and controllable emphasis without compromising naturalness. To address these issues, we propose QFocus, a framework for automated prosodic emphasis editing that enables precise control of emphasis positions in speech synthesis. By integrating an Emphasis Gating Network (EGN) and emphasis-aware loss functions, QFocus effectively embeds stress cues into semantic distributions and supervises prosodic features of emphasized parts. Experiments on both in-domain and out-of-domain benchmarks demonstrate that QFocus achieves more accurate and expressive emphasis generation without compromising speech quality, surpassing state-of-the-art baselines such as CosyVoice2 and EME-TTS, while requiring only a small amount of annotated data and demonstrating strong generalization.
